Who I Am I bring heart to the table, but I also bring grit. Whether I am researching justice-related issues, writing about human experiences, or working on advocacy efforts that matter to families and children, I care about substance. I want my work to matter in the real world, not just on paper. What Drives Me I care deeply about overlooked people, difficult truths, and the stories that too often go unheard. I am especially drawn to work involving children, victims, identity, dignity, and family connection. I believe compassion is powerful, but compassion paired with action is where real change starts. Education My academic journey reflects both discipline and purpose. I approach learning as a tool for advocacy, analysis, and service. Advocacy Advocacy is not just one part of what I do. It is woven into how I think, write, research, and live. Family-Centered Advocacy I care deeply about the emotional well-being of children and the importance of healthy, loving family bonds. My advocacy work centers on fairness, continuity, and protecting meaningful relationships that help children feel safe, connected, and loved. Victim and Child Welfare Focus I am passionate about helping those who are vulnerable, unheard, or harmed by systems that fail to fully see them. That includes children, victims, and families navigating difficult realities they did not create. Voice Through Writing I use writing as a form of advocacy. Whether the work is academic, personal, or creative, I believe words can document truth, preserve dignity, and move people toward empathy and action. Change With Substance I care about advocacy that is thoughtful, informed, and grounded. Real change needs more than noise. It needs evidence, persistence, strategy, and the courage to keep going when something matters. Research & Projects I am drawn to research that digs deeper, asks better questions, and looks beyond surface-level answers. Criminal Justice Research My interests include criminal justice systems, victims, behavioral patterns, social issues, and investigative work that helps uncover truth. I enjoy the process of examining details, connecting evidence, and thinking critically about what is really happening beneath the surface. Mission-Driven Projects I value projects that do more than satisfy a requirement. I am most energized by work that informs, supports, or protects people. Research becomes meaningful to me when it can be used to help someone, challenge assumptions, or contribute to understanding in a practical way. Writing & Creative Work Writing is one of the strongest threads running through everything I do. I write to express, to connect, to advocate, and to leave something meaningful behind. Personal Writing I write from a place of honesty, emotion, and reflection. My writing often explores resilience, love, grief, identity, justice, and the bonds that shape who we are. Children’s Stories I am deeply drawn to storytelling that brings comfort, warmth, imagination, and heart. Children’s writing allows me to create meaningful worlds filled with love, wonder, and hope. Academic and Research Writing I also enjoy writing that is analytical, structured, and evidence-based. I appreciate the discipline of building a clear argument while keeping the human stakes at the center of the work. Storytelling With Purpose Whether I am writing a paper, a poem, a project, or a story, I want it to carry something real. I do not want empty words. I want truth, depth, and connection.
