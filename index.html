About

I’m JoAnn Bishop, a student, advocate, researcher, and writer committed to protecting the overlooked and giving voice to stories that matter. My work blends criminal justice, human services, advocacy, investigative curiosity, and storytelling with heart, purpose, and persistence.

I believe truth matters. Connection matters. Children matter. Families matter. The stories people carry matter. Whether I am writing, researching, or advocating, I want my work to leave something meaningful behind.

Advocacy & Research

Advocacy

My advocacy centers on children, families, victims, and those whose pain is too often minimized or ignored. I care deeply about fairness, emotional safety, dignity, and protecting meaningful human connection.

Research

My research interests include criminal justice systems, investigative analysis, victim-centered work, behavioral patterns, and deeper truths that often sit beneath the surface.

Student Work

My academic work connects criminal justice, human services, and advocacy to real-world problems that affect communities, vulnerable populations, and systems of justice.

Mission-Driven Projects

I am most drawn to work that informs, protects, helps, or uncovers truth. I care about projects that carry substance and real human impact.

Writing

Writing is one of the strongest threads running through everything I do. I write to advocate, to comfort, to tell the truth, to connect, and to leave something meaningful behind.

My work includes academic writing, personal reflection, mission-driven storytelling, and children’s books filled with warmth, imagination, and heart.

Mission

My mission is to combine compassion, research, advocacy, and storytelling to honor truth, protect connection, and make sure overlooked voices are not forgotten.
