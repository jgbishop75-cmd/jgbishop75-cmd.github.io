About

I’m JoAnn Bishop, a student, advocate, researcher, and writer driven by a core belief: people deserve to be seen, heard, and protected. My work brings together criminal justice, human services, advocacy, storytelling, and research in ways that center truth, dignity, and real human impact.

I care deeply about overlooked people, difficult truths, and the stories too often ignored. My goal is to combine knowledge with compassion and action—using research, writing, and advocacy to create change.

Education

Southern New Hampshire University – Academic focus in Criminal Justice, Human Services, Advocacy, and Research.

My studies center on systems analysis, justice reform, victim support, and strengthening communities through informed advocacy and evidence-based research.

Advocacy

Advocacy is not just part of my work—it is woven into how I think and live. My advocacy work focuses on fairness, dignity, and protecting meaningful relationships that help children feel safe, connected, and loved.

Research

My research interests include criminal justice systems, victim support, behavioral patterns, investigative work, and social issues affecting families and communities.

Research allows us to ask better questions, challenge assumptions, and move closer to truth.

Writing

Writing is both advocacy and storytelling. Through academic writing, research papers, and creative work, I aim to bring attention to difficult issues while also offering hope and understanding.
